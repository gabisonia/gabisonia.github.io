Most engineering content over-indexes on tools and under-explains tradeoffs.

What usually matters more is the reasoning around boundaries, ownership, reliability, delivery speed, and how a team keeps complexity from spreading faster than the product grows.

That is the center of this blog.

You can expect posts about:

- backend and platform architecture
- .NET systems and service design
- refactoring strategies for existing products
- engineering management from a technical perspective
- code review, testing, and release discipline

The goal is not volume. The goal is to publish things that remain useful six months later.
